Product Catalogue

A digital storefront for merchants. An order flow for customers. Payment on Paper.id's rails.

Writer Jane Radianti Sugianto
Reviewers Brian Ridwan, Rinaldi Limeo, Rishi Bhansali
BU RNG · Squad 2
Created June 2026
🎯

The bet

Paper.id currently enters a transaction after the order has already happened. A seller receives an order on WhatsApp, creates an invoice manually, and shares a bank account number. The payment goes off-platform. Paper.id never sees it.

Product Catalogue moves Paper.id upstream — from the invoice step to the order step. The seller builds a catalogue once and publishes it to a unique storefront URL. Buyers browse and submit orders directly. The seller confirms. Paper.id auto-generates the invoice and fires the payment link. The order happens inside Paper.id, so the payment has nowhere to go except Paper.id's rails.

This is the same move Square made with the card reader: give small businesses a free point-of-sale tool to own where the transaction starts. Once the sale happens on Square, the payment follows. We are doing the same thing one layer up.

🔍

The problem

  • 💬
    No lightweight order capture toolMerchants have no simple way to share products and receive orders without requiring customers to create a Paper.id account.
  • 📄
    Manual order-to-invoice workflowsMerchants manually create Sales Orders and Sales Invoices after every order — causing delays, errors, and reconciliation overhead.
  • 🛒
    No self-serve buying experienceCustomers rely entirely on offline communication (WhatsApp, phone) to browse products and place orders.
  • 💸
    Fragmented payment reconciliationPayments are not automatically linked to their originating Sales Order and Invoice — requiring manual matching.
  • 📉
    Missed retention opportunityWithout an embedded order and payment flow, Paper.id misses becoming the primary operational system for merchants' sales cycles.
👥

Who it's for

M

Merchant (User / Admin)

A Paper.id account holder who builds and manages the catalogue, creates orders on behalf of customers, reviews and confirms incoming orders, and oversees the full order-to-payment cycle.

Target: mid-sized B2B sellers in F&B, construction, textile, and health & beauty with a stable, repeat customer base.

C

Customer

The end buyer. No Paper.id account needed. Receives a catalogue link from the merchant via WhatsApp, browses, selects items, and submits an order with just their name and phone number.

They are not discovering the merchant on Paper.id — the merchant sent them the link.

⚡

What we're building

Phase 0 delivers two order creation flows on top of a shared product catalogue. In both flows: the merchant confirms before any invoice or payment link is generated.

Flow A

Merchant-initiated order

Built for repeat B2B — the order is already known

1Merchant builds order from catalogue
2Applies discount & fees, confirms
3Sends order link to customer via WhatsApp
4Customer reviews & confirms
5Customer pays via auto-generated link
✓Auto SO → SI → PayIn, reconciled
Flow B

Customer self-serve order

Built for inbound — buyer browses on their own

1Customer opens storefront URL (no login)
2Browses, adds to cart, submits order
3Merchant reviews & confirms
4Payment link auto-sent to customer
5Customer pays
✓Auto SO → SI → PayIn, reconciled

What we're not building

✕
A marketplace. Buyers cannot discover sellers on Paper.id. The catalogue is only reachable via a link the seller shares.
✕
Inventory management. Availability is a binary on/off toggle per product. No stock counters, no quantity tracking — the merchant manually toggles a product off when it is unavailable.
✕
Direct checkout. The seller confirms every order before the invoice fires. SMB commerce in Indonesia is negotiated.
✕
MOQ enforcement. Not in this phase. See Good to Have.

A. Merchant flows 5 tickets · Each maps to one ClickUp ticket
A-1 Create and manage product catalogue ▾
User story

As a Paper.id merchant, I want to create and manage a product catalogue inside Paper.id, so I can list my products with prices and make them available for ordering.

Entry pointDashboard → Product Catalogue (new left nav)
Context Today, merchants manage product lists outside Paper.id — WhatsApp messages, spreadsheets, printed price lists.
1 · Access & navigation
  • Product Catalogue accessible from the left navigation menu
  • Empty state shows a clear CTA to add the first product
2 · Add product
  • Fields: Product Name (required, max 100 chars), Description (optional, max 500 chars), Price (required, IDR, > 0), Product Image (optional, JPG/PNG, max 5MB), SKU (optional), Unit (optional, e.g. pcs, box, kg)
  • Required fields validated on submission; inline error shown on failure
  • Product appears in the catalogue list immediately on save
3 · Edit & delete product
  • Merchant can edit any product field; changes reflect immediately across the catalogue and storefront
  • Delete triggers a confirmation modal before removal
  • Deleted products are removed from both the merchant list and the public storefront URL
4 · Storefront URL generation
  • Unique storefront URL auto-generated when the first product is saved
  • URL is permanent per merchant account unless manually reset
  • Merchant can copy the URL from the Product Catalogue module
  • URL leads to the customer-facing catalogue page — no login required for buyers
5 · Edge cases
  • Price ≤ 0 → inline error: "Price must be greater than 0"
  • Image upload failure → product saves without image; non-blocking warning shown
  • Catalogue must support ≥ 100 products without performance degradation

A-2 Create order on behalf of customer ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want to select products from my catalogue and build an order for a specific customer, so I can capture the order quickly without the customer needing to browse the catalogue themselves.

Entry pointProduct Catalogue module → Create Order
1 · Product selection
  • Merchant can browse, search (by name or SKU), and add products from their catalogue
  • Minimum quantity per line item: 1
  • Merchant can adjust quantities or remove items before confirming
2 · Cart review
  • Cart shows: product name, SKU (if set), unit price, quantity, line total; subtotal; discount (if applied); custom fees (if added); grand total
  • Cart persists within the same session if the merchant navigates away
3 · Discount
  • Merchant enters a discount as a free-form IDR amount or percentage — open input field, no structured discount types
  • Discount cannot exceed the subtotal → error: "Discount cannot exceed order subtotal"
  • Discount shown as a separate line item in the cart summary
4 · Custom & delivery fees
  • Merchant can add one or more fee lines, each with a label (required) and IDR amount (required, > 0)
  • Each fee appears as a separate line item; all fees included in grand total
  • Merchant can remove any fee line before confirming
5 · Order confirmation
  • Before confirming, merchant enters: Customer Name (required), Customer WhatsApp Number (required, validated for WA format)
  • Final order summary shown before tapping "Confirm Order"
  • On confirm, a draft Sales Order is staged and linked to the merchant's account
6 · Edge cases
  • Empty cart on confirm → "Please add at least one product to create an order"
  • Invalid WA number → "Please enter a valid WhatsApp number"
  • Product price changes after being added to cart → cart updates to latest price with notice: "Price for [Product Name] has been updated"

A-3 Send order to customer via WhatsApp ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want to send a confirmed order to my customer via WhatsApp, so they can review and pay without me typing out the order details manually.

Entry pointOrder confirmation screen → "Send via WhatsApp" or "Copy Link"
1 · Send via WhatsApp
  • Opens wa.me with the customer's number pre-filled and a pre-composed message: merchant/business name, order summary (products, quantities, total), and the unique order link
  • Merchant can edit the message before sending
  • No WA Business API required for Phase 0
2 · Copy link
  • "Copy Link" copies the unique order link to clipboard
  • Success toast: "Order link copied to clipboard"
3 · Order link behaviour
  • Link is unique per order; directs the customer to an order summary with a "Confirm & Pay" action
  • Link is valid until the order is paid or cancelled
  • Already paid: "This order has already been completed. Thank you!"
  • Cancelled: "This order is no longer available"
4 · Resend
  • Merchant can resend or re-copy the order link from the order detail page at any time
5 · Edge cases
  • WhatsApp not detected on device → fall back to Copy Link with notice: "WhatsApp not detected. Please copy the link and send manually"
  • Expired or invalid order link → customer sees an error page with support contact

A-4 Review and confirm incoming customer order ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want to review orders submitted by customers via my public storefront, apply any adjustments, and confirm before the invoice fires — so I stay in control of pricing and terms.

Entry pointIn-app notification → Order detail page
1 · Order notification
  • Merchant receives an in-app notification when a customer submits a self-serve order
  • Notification shows: customer name, phone number, items ordered, and order total
2 · Order review
  • Merchant can view the full order: line items, quantities, prices, customer notes
  • Merchant can apply a discount via a free-form IDR or % input field before confirming
  • Merchant can add custom or delivery fees before confirming
3 · Confirm order
  • On confirm, Paper.id auto-generates the invoice and sends a payment link directly to the customer's phone number
  • Order status in the merchant dashboard updates to "Awaiting Payment"
4 · Reject / cancel order
  • Merchant can reject an unconfirmed order
  • Customer's order link updates to: "This order is no longer available"
5 · Edge cases
  • If the merchant does not confirm within [TBD] hours, a reminder notification is sent
  • If the merchant's account is suspended, the storefront shows: "This catalogue is temporarily unavailable" and new orders cannot be received

A-5 Auto document creation & auto-reconciliation ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want SO, SI, and PayIn to be automatically created and linked when my customer pays, so I never have to manually create documents or reconcile payments.

Entry pointTriggered automatically on successful payment (Flow A & B)
1 · Trigger
  • Fires when the customer successfully completes payment via the payment link
  • No merchant action required
2 · Document creation (within 30s of payment)
  • Sales Order (SO): reflects items, quantities, prices, discount, fees
  • Sales Invoice (SI): generated from the SO with payment terms and due date
  • PayIn: records the payment received, linked to the SI
  • All three documents are interlinked: SO → SI → PayIn
3 · Auto-reconciliation
  • PayIn automatically reconciled against SI — no manual matching required
  • SI status → "Paid" · SO status → "Completed / Fulfilled"
4 · Merchant dashboard
  • SO, SI, and PayIn appear in the merchant dashboard immediately after payment
  • Each document links to the related documents
  • Order status in the Product Catalogue order list → "Paid"
5 · Payment notification
  • In-app notification: "Payment received for Order #[ID] — [Name] paid IDR [AMOUNT]"
  • Push/email notification per the merchant's notification preferences
6 · Edge cases
  • Document creation failure after payment → payment is still recorded; system retries up to 3 times; merchant alerted if all retries fail
  • Insufficient permissions to create SO/SI → system error shown; support team notified
  • Duplicate payment attempt → handled by payment gateway; only one PayIn created per order

B. Customer flows 3 tickets · Each maps to one ClickUp ticket
B-1 Access storefront (no login required) ▾
User story

As a customer of a Paper.id merchant, I want to open the merchant's catalogue via a shared link without creating an account, so I can browse and order quickly.

Entry pointMerchant's storefront URL (shared via WhatsApp or any channel)
Context Today, customers have no digital way to browse a merchant's products. All ordering happens via WhatsApp chat or phone, leading to miscommunication and delays.
1 · Catalogue access
  • Opens in any mobile or desktop browser — no login required to browse. Login is deferred to order submission to keep the top-of-funnel frictionless.
  • Displays merchant's business name, logo (if set), and product list
  • Mobile-responsive, optimised for WhatsApp-referred traffic (majority on mobile)
2 · Catalogue display
  • Each product shows: image (or placeholder), name, description (if set), price (IDR with thousand separators), unit (if set)
  • Paginated or infinite scroll if > 20 products
3 · State handling
  • No products: "This catalogue is currently empty. Please contact the seller"
  • Invalid URL / inactive account: "This catalogue is not available. Please contact the seller"
  • Suspended account: "This catalogue is temporarily unavailable"
4 · Performance
  • Page loads within 3 seconds on standard 4G mobile
  • Images are lazy-loaded to avoid blocking initial page render

B-2 Add products to cart & review order ▾
User story

As a customer, I want to add products to my cart and review my order before confirming, so I know I have the right items and quantities before sending it to the merchant.

Entry pointStorefront page → Add to Cart → Cart review page
1 · Adding to cart
  • Customer can add any product; minimum quantity 1, maximum 9,999 (or merchant-defined)
  • Cart badge updates in real time
  • Customer can adjust quantities or remove items directly from the cart
2 · Cart review
  • Shows: product name, image (if available), unit price, quantity, line total; subtotal; any merchant-added fees (read-only); grand total
  • Prices are fixed and read-only — customer cannot modify prices
  • Empty cart: "Your cart is empty. Go back to browse products"
3 · Edge cases
  • Product price or availability changes while in cart → cart updates to latest; notice: "[Product Name] has been updated. Please review your cart"
  • Product removed from catalogue while in cart → item flagged as "No longer available"; customer must remove flagged items before submitting

B-3 Submit order & complete payment ▾
User story

As a customer, I want to submit my order to the merchant and pay once they confirm it, so I can complete my purchase without any back-and-forth.

Entry pointCart review → Confirm Order → Post-submission → Payment (after merchant confirms)
1 · Order submission
  • Customer enters: Name (required), Phone Number (required), Notes/Remarks (optional, max 300 chars)
  • No login or OTP required — phone number is collected as part of the order form, not as an authentication step
  • Customer sees the final order summary before submitting
  • On submit, order is sent to the merchant as a pending order notification
2 · Post-submission state
  • Customer sees: "Your order has been submitted. The merchant will review and confirm it shortly."
  • After submission, optionally prompt: "Verify your number to track this order and see future orders." — one tap, skippable. This is the only point where OTP is offered, and it is never required.
  • Phone number is stored against the order regardless of whether the customer verifies — enables order history in Phase 1 without re-collecting data
4 · Payment (after merchant confirms)
  • Once the merchant confirms, Paper.id auto-sends a payment link to the customer's phone number
  • Customer pays via methods configured in the merchant's Paper.id account
  • Success: "Payment successful! Your order has been confirmed"
  • Failure: "Payment failed. Please try again or use a different payment method" — customer can retry with the same link
5 · Payment link validity
  • Valid for 24 hours by default (or per the merchant's configured due date)
  • Expired link: "This payment link has expired. Please contact the merchant"
6 · Edge cases
  • Browser closed before payment → order stays "Pending Payment"; customer can reopen via the order link
  • Duplicate payment attempt → "This order has already been paid"
  • Payment gateway unavailable → "Payment service is currently unavailable. Please try again in a few minutes"
  • VA refund (edge case): If a customer completes a Virtual Account bank transfer and the order is subsequently cancelled, the refund cannot be processed programmatically — VA transfers are not reversible via the payment gateway. Resolution requires manual intervention by the Paper.id ops team. CC payments can be refunded directly to the card via the payment gateway with no manual step.

Good to have — validate demand first

These are directionally useful but add meaningful complexity. Do not commit them to a phase until Phase 0 data shows merchants actually need them.

  • Bulk order discounts and layered pricing per customer tier or industry Validate first
    Complicates the order confirmation flow and pricing model significantly. Worth adding only if data shows repeat B2B merchants are blocked without it.
  • Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ) enforcement per product Validate first
    Adds friction to the customer cart experience. Only relevant for distributors with strict wholesale minimums — a small subset of Phase 0 merchants.
