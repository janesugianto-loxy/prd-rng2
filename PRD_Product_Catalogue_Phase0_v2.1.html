Product Catalogue

A digital storefront for merchants. An order flow for customers. Payment on Paper.id's rails.

Writer Jane Radianti Sugianto
Reviewers Brian Ridwan, Rinaldi Limeo, Rishi Bhansali
BU RNG · Squad 2
Created June 2026
🎯

The bet

Paper.id currently enters a transaction after the order has already happened. A seller receives an order on WhatsApp, creates an invoice manually, and shares a bank account number. The payment goes off-platform. Paper.id never sees it.

Product Catalogue moves Paper.id upstream — from the invoice step to the order step. The seller builds a catalogue once and publishes it to a unique storefront URL. Buyers browse and submit orders directly. The seller confirms. Paper.id auto-generates the invoice and fires the payment link. The order happens inside Paper.id, so the payment has nowhere to go except Paper.id's rails.

This is the same move Square made with the card reader: give small businesses a free point-of-sale tool to own where the transaction starts. Once the sale happens on Square, the payment follows. We are doing the same thing one layer up.

This gap is especially pronounced for B2C sellers, who still handle sales the old way outside the platform — orders taken on WhatsApp, manually noted, with no structured way to capture or track them. Paper.id's B2B capability, Sales Invoice, doesn't fit this segment, and PO Form and Compact Invoice aren't yet seamless for it either: we already see users who use Payout but can't use PayIn simply because their business model doesn't match our B2B-oriented flow.

Product Catalogue closes this gap, bringing B2C merchants onto the platform for collection, not just disbursement — via two patterns: self-serve catalogue ordering (customer browses and picks, like a WhatsApp catalogue) and admin-assisted ordering (merchant inputs the order, customer just pays, like Compact Invoice).

🔍

The problem

  • 💬
    No lightweight order capture toolMerchants have no simple way to share products and receive orders without requiring customers to create a Paper.id account.
  • 📄
    Manual order-to-invoice workflowsMerchants manually create Sales Orders and Sales Invoices after every order — causing delays, errors, and reconciliation overhead.
  • 🛒
    No self-serve buying experienceCustomers rely entirely on offline communication (WhatsApp, phone) to browse products and place orders.
  • 💸
    Fragmented payment reconciliationPayments are not automatically linked to their originating Sales Order and Invoice — requiring manual matching.
  • 📉
    Missed retention opportunityWithout an embedded order and payment flow, Paper.id misses becoming the primary operational system for merchants' sales cycles.
📦

About this module

Product Catalogue is focused on mid-sized B2C sellers. It's built by dividing and enhancing capabilities we already have across two existing modules — PO Form and Compact Invoice — bringing them together into one cohesive catalogue-to-order experience rather than two disconnected pieces, both from the customer and merchant side in Paper.id.

They are not discovering the merchant on Paper.id — the merchant sent them the link.

⚡

What we're building

Phase 0 delivers two order creation flows on top of a shared product catalogue. In both flows: payment is captured as soon as the customer pays — there is no merchant gate before checkout. The Sales Invoice (SI) and PayIn are created automatically the moment payment lands. The merchant then has 30 minutes from payment to confirm or cancel the order; there is no auto-confirm — if the window closes with no action, the system auto-cancels and refunds in full. See Order & payment status reference for the complete breakdown.

💳

Payment flow

Payment is captured immediately when the customer pays — there is no merchant gate before checkout. The SI and PayIn are created automatically the moment payment lands. The merchant then has 30 minutes from payment to confirm or cancel the order; there is no auto-confirm, and if the window closes unconfirmed, the system auto-cancels and refunds in full. A merchant can still decline an order before the customer has paid, if needed — nothing has been charged yet, so there's nothing to refund. See Order & payment status reference for every event in the lifecycle.

Credit Card

Charged on payment

Captured immediately — no hold-and-capture-on-confirm

1Customer enters CC details and pays → charge captured immediately, Payment Status → "Paid"
2Merchant confirms within 30 minutes of payment → Order Status → "Confirmed"
✕Merchant cancels while still within the settlement SLA (funds not yet in Active Balance) → auto-refunded via the gateway, SI & PayIn deleted
!Merchant cancels after the charge has already settled into Active Balance → same as VA below: stays "Paid" until a manual refund
✕30-minute window expires unconfirmed → auto-cancelled, refunded in full, SI & PayIn deleted
Virtual Account

Instant to the balance

VA number generated immediately — no wait for merchant confirmation

1Customer selects VA → number generated right away, no expiry while awaiting transfer
2Customer transfers → payment confirmed instantly, Payment Status → "Paid"
3Merchant confirms within 30 minutes of payment → Order Status → "Confirmed"
!Merchant cancels after payment → manual refund via Paper.id ops. VA transfers are not programmatically reversible, so it stays "Paid" until refunded and marked so.
✕30-minute window expires unconfirmed → auto-cancelled, refunded in full, SI & PayIn deleted (same outcome as CC)
The 30-minute window only starts once payment is received — not at order submission, and choosing a payment method has no expiry of its own. There is no auto-confirm: the merchant must actively confirm or cancel. If the window closes with no action, the system auto-cancels and refunds the customer in full, and both parties are notified via WhatsApp.

A-1 Create and manage product catalogue ▾
User story

As a Paper.id merchant, I want to create and manage a product catalogue inside Paper.id, so I can list my products with prices and make them available for ordering.

Entry pointDashboard → Product Catalogue (new left nav)
Context Today, merchants manage product lists outside Paper.id — WhatsApp messages, spreadsheets, printed price lists.
1 · Access & navigation
  • Product Catalogue accessible from the left navigation menu
  • Empty state shows a clear CTA to add the first product
2 · Add product
  • Fields: Product Name (required, max 100 chars), Description (optional, max 500 chars), Price (required, IDR, > 0), Product Image (optional, JPG/PNG, max 5MB), SKU (optional), Unit (optional, e.g. pcs, box, kg)
  • Required fields validated on submission; inline error shown on failure
  • Product appears in the catalogue list immediately on save
3 · Edit & delete product
  • Merchant can edit any product field; changes reflect immediately across the catalogue and storefront
  • Delete triggers a confirmation modal before removal
  • Deleted products are removed from both the merchant list and the public storefront URL
4 · Storefront URL generation
  • Unique storefront URL auto-generated when the first product is saved
  • URL is permanent per merchant account unless manually reset
  • Merchant can copy the URL from the Product Catalogue module
  • URL leads to the customer-facing catalogue page — no login required for buyers
5 · Store settings
  • Require delivery address — toggle in store settings (Owner only). On by default. When turned off, the shipping address field is hidden from the customer order form — for merchants who operate pick-up only or deliver to a fixed location. Phase 1: the field itself (and this toggle) is not yet built in the Phase 0 prototype — see B-3 for what Phase 0 actually collects.
  • Setting applies store-wide; individual product-level overrides are Phase 1.
6 · Edge cases
  • Price ≤ 0 → inline error: "Price must be greater than 0"
  • Image upload failure → product saves without image; non-blocking warning shown
  • Catalogue must support ≥ 100 products without performance degradation

A-2 Create order on behalf of customer ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want to select products from my catalogue and build an order for a specific customer, so I can capture the order quickly without the customer needing to browse the catalogue themselves.

Entry pointProduct Catalogue module → Create Order
1 · Product selection
  • Merchant can browse, search (by name or SKU), and add products from their catalogue
  • Minimum quantity per line item: 1
  • Merchant can adjust quantities or remove items before confirming
2 · Cart review
  • Cart shows: product name, SKU (if set), unit price, quantity, line total; subtotal; custom fields (if added — see below); grand total
  • Cart persists within the same session if the merchant navigates away
3 · Discount & other adjustments (updated)
  • There is no separate fixed "discount" field. Discounts, fees, and any other adjustment all use the same generic custom-field mechanism — see A-10.

  • Invalid WA number → "Please enter a valid WhatsApp number"

A-5 Auto document creation & auto-reconciliation ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want SI and PayIn to be automatically created and linked when my customer pays, so I never have to manually create documents or reconcile payments.

Entry pointTriggered automatically on successful payment (Flow A & B)
1 · Trigger
  • Fires when the customer successfully completes payment — at checkout (Flow B) or via the order link (Flow A)
  • No merchant action required. This happens before the merchant confirms — see A-4
2 · Document creation (within 30s of payment) (updated)
  • Sales Invoice (SI): generated with items, quantities, prices, custom fields (A-10), payment terms, and due date
  • PayIn: records the payment received, linked to the SI
  • The two documents are interlinked: SI → PayIn. No Sales Order document is created in Phase 0.
3 · Auto-reconciliation
  • PayIn automatically reconciled against SI — no manual matching required
  • SI status → "Paid"
4 · Merchant dashboard
  • SI and PayIn appear in the merchant dashboard immediately after payment
  • Each document links to the related documents
  • Payment Status in the Product Catalogue order list → "Paid". Order Status stays "Pending" until the merchant confirms or cancels (A-4) — the two are tracked separately, see Order & payment status reference
5 · Payment notification
  • In-app notification: "Payment received for Order #[ID] — [Name] paid IDR [AMOUNT]"
  • Push/email notification per the merchant's notification preferences
6 · Edge cases
  • Document creation failure after payment → payment is still recorded; system retries up to 3 times; merchant alerted if all retries fail
  • Insufficient permissions to create the SI → system error shown; support team notified
  • Duplicate payment attempt → handled by payment gateway; only one PayIn created per order
7 · Cancellation & refund reconciliation (updated)
  • Auto-cancelled (30-min confirmation window elapses unconfirmed): Payment Status → "Refunded". The auto-generated SI is deleted, not reverted to "Unpaid" — it should never have existed as a billable document. The PayIn record is deleted too.
  • Merchant cancels a CC order still within the settlement SLA (funds not yet credited to the merchant's Active Balance): same as above — Payment Status → "Refunded", SI deleted, PayIn deleted. The refund is programmatic via the payment gateway, no manual step.
  • Merchant cancels a CC order that has already settled into Active Balance, or any VA order: funds have genuinely arrived, so Payment Status stays "Paid", SI stays "Paid", and the PayIn record is left untouched. Requires a manual refund by Paper.id ops.
  • Merchant marks a manual refund complete: Payment Status → "Refunded", SI reverts to "Unpaid" (stays on record — it isn't deleted, since real money did move). The PayIn record is left unchanged.
  • Net effect: SI/PayIn are only ever deleted when no money actually settled with the merchant; whenever real funds moved, the documents stay on record and simply change status.

A-8 Invoice & tax handling New ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want my auto-generated invoices to correctly apply the right tax per product so my records are compliant and I do not have to manually adjust invoices after they are sent.

1 · Tax classifications per product
  • PPN 11% — standard VAT on goods and most taxable services. Applied on top of product price.
  • Pajak Daerah (custom %) — regional tax entered as a custom percentage by the merchant. Common for F&B (Pajak Restoran, typically 10%). Rate varies by region and service type.
  • Non-taxable (exempt) — education fees (jasa pendidikan), certain medical and health services, and any product where no tax applies.
2 · Auto-invoice calculation rules
  • Tax calculated per line item based on the product's tax classification
  • Custom fields (including any discount, entered as a negative field — see A-10) applied to subtotal before tax is calculated
  • Each tax type shown as its own line on the invoice — PPN and Pajak Daerah never blended
  • Invoice total = subtotal ± custom fields (A-10) + all tax lines
  • PKP merchants: PPN invoices flagged for e-Faktur — e-Faktur generation is Phase 1
3 · Examples

F&B Restoran — Pajak Daerah 10%

Es Teh Manis × 2Rp 20,000

  • No tax classification set → defaults to Non-taxable; warning badge shown on product in catalogue management
  • Pajak Daerah rate left blank → product cannot be saved: "Please enter the applicable tax rate"
  • Mixed-tax order (PPN + Pajak Daerah in same cart) → both tax lines shown separately. No cross-tax blending.

A-10 Custom order fields (discounts, fees & other adjustments) New ▾
User story

As a merchant, I want to add my own labelled charges or deductions to an order — not just a fixed "discount" or "tax" field — so I can reflect however my business actually adjusts a price (shipping fee, packaging fee, loyalty discount, rounding adjustment, etc.).

Entry pointOrder builder (A-2) → "Add field" → applies to order total → reflected in customer's order review (B-2/B-3)
Context Phase 0 originally scoped a fixed discount/tax field as Phase 1. Instead of hard-coding fixed field types, this ticket builds one generic, merchant-editable custom field mechanism that covers discounts, fees, and any other named adjustment a merchant needs — avoiding a rebuild later when Phase 1's tiered pricing/tax work begins.
1 · Adding a custom field
  • Merchant can add one or more custom fields to an order from the order builder (Flow A) or catalogue-level defaults (applies to every order)
  • Each field has: Label (free text, editable, max 30 chars — e.g. "Discount", "Shipping fee", "Loyalty credit") and Value (numeric input, IDR, positive or negative)
  • Merchant chooses whether the field adds to or subtracts from the order subtotal (toggle: + / −)
  • Multiple custom fields can be added to the same order; each appears as its own line
2 · Calculation & display
  • Order total = subtotal ± sum of all custom field values
  • Each custom field line is shown by its label and value in: the merchant's order builder, the customer's order review (cart/checkout), the generated Sales Invoice, and the PayIn amount
  • Values are read-only to the customer — only the merchant can add, edit, or remove fields
3 · Edge cases
  • Field value left blank or non-numeric → "Enter a valid amount", blocks order submission
  • Field would make the order total negative → "Total can't be less than Rp 0. Adjust your fields."
  • Merchant edits or removes a field after the customer has already submitted the order but before confirming → customer sees an updated total notice before payment link is sent
  • Label left blank → defaults to "Adjustment"

B-3 Choose payment method & pay (updated) ▾
User story

As a customer, I want to pay for my order right after verifying my number, so I don't have to wait for the merchant before I can complete my purchase.

Entry pointOTP verification (B-4) success → Payment method screen → Pay → Receipt
Note There is no waiting-for-merchant step here, and no separate order-form page. Once OTP succeeds, the customer goes straight to choosing a payment method and paying — the merchant's confirm/cancel decision (A-4) happens afterward, within 30 minutes of payment.
1 · Payment method selection
  • Customer chooses Credit Card or Virtual Account (or other methods the merchant has enabled)
  • Shows a billed-amount summary: subtotal, any custom fields applied (A-10), service fee, and total
  • No expiry on this screen — the customer can take as long as they need to choose and pay (see Order & payment status reference, row 1)
2 · Payment
  • Credit Card is charged immediately on submission; Virtual Account is credited the moment the customer completes the bank transfer
  • Either way, Payment Status → "Paid" the instant it succeeds, and the SI + PayIn are created automatically (A-5) — before the merchant has done anything
  • Success: "Payment successful! Your order has been received"
  • Failure: "Payment failed. Please try again or use a different payment method" — customer can retry
3 · Receipt & what happens next
  • Customer sees a receipt with order summary, total paid, and: "[Merchant] will confirm your order within 30 minutes."
  • Name and phone are already verified (B-4) — no further identity step needed
  • Shipping address: Phase 1. The Phase 0 prototype collects name, phone, and order notes only (captured earlier, on B-2) — no address, delivery notes, or geolocation field yet. Once built, this will be entered before payment, alongside the fields already on B-2.
4 · Order/receipt link validity
  • After paying, the customer can reopen their order/receipt link for 24 hours to check status or re-download the receipt
  • After 24 hours the link expires: "This link has expired. Please contact the merchant"
5 · Edge cases
  • Browser closed before payment → order stays at the payment method screen; customer can reopen and continue, no time limit
  • Duplicate payment attempt → "This order has already been paid"
  • Payment gateway unavailable → "Payment service is currently unavailable. Please try again in a few minutes"
  • VA refund (edge case): If a customer completes a Virtual Account bank transfer and the order is subsequently cancelled, the refund cannot be processed programmatically — VA transfers are not reversible via the payment gateway. Resolution requires manual intervention by the Paper.id ops team; the Sales Invoice stays "Paid" until the refund is completed, then reverts to "Unpaid" (it is not deleted, since the funds genuinely arrived). CC payments still within the settlement SLA (not yet in the merchant's Active Balance) can be refunded directly via the payment gateway with no manual step — in that case the Sales Invoice and PayIn are deleted outright rather than reverted, since no funds ever actually settled. A CC payment that has already settled follows the same manual-refund path as VA. See A-5 · Cancellation & refund reconciliation for the full breakdown.

Order & payment status reference

Order Status (the merchant's approval workflow) and Payment Status (the money) are tracked separately. This table walks through every event in the order lifecycle and what happens to each of the four things the merchant can see: Order Status, Payment Status, the Sales Invoice, and the payment transaction list entry (PaperPay In). Rows 4 and 6a are highlighted — these are the cases where the Sales Invoice and payment record are deleted outright rather than reverted, since no funds ever actually settled with the merchant.

# Event / trigger Order Status Payment Status Sales Invoice Payment Transaction List Notes
1 Customer is on the "choose payment method" page Pending Awaiting Payment Not yet created Not yet created Only appears here. No expiry — the customer can take as long as they need.
2 Payment completes (CC charged or VA transferred) Pending Paid Created — Paid Created — on record The 30-minute confirm clock starts only now — not at order submission.
3 Merchant confirms within 30 min Confirmed Paid Stays Paid Stays on record No auto-confirm — only happens on explicit merchant action.
4 30 minutes pass with no merchant action Cancelled (auto) Refunded Deleted Deleted System auto-cancels and refunds in full — same outcome for CC and VA.
5 Merchant declines before the customer has paid Cancelled Unpaid Never created Never created Nothing was charged, so nothing to refund.
6a Merchant cancels a paid CC order — still within the settlement SLA (funds not yet in Active Balance) Cancelled Refunded Deleted Deleted Instant refund via the gateway — no manual step.
6b Merchant cancels a paid CC order — already settled into Active Balance Cancelled Paid (unchanged) Stays Paid Stays on record Behaves exactly like a VA cancellation (row 7) — a settled charge can't be reversed automatically.
7 Merchant cancels a paid VA order Cancelled Paid (unchanged) Stays Paid Stays on record VA funds are instant to the balance, so this is always the outcome for VA — never the 6a path.
8 Merchant marks a cancelled order "Refunded" after completing the manual refund (applies to 6b and 7) Cancelled Refunded Reverts to Unpaid Unchanged, stays on record Not deleted here — the money genuinely arrived and left via a separate manual refund, so it stays for the books.
9 Merchant marks a Confirmed order delivered Done Paid Stays Paid Stays on record Terminal status, set manually — no further changes expected.
